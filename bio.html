Biography

Samantha Orr began studying the piano at age 3 and the violin at age 4. She quickly became impressed with the power of communication that music can lend. She began studying with the Kitchener Waterloo Community String School and discovered the joy of collaborative playing.

Samantha soon found she couldn't get enough of music, she joined the Georgian Bay Symphony Youth Orchestra as well as the Georgian Bay Symphony.

Competing in kiwanis festivals in Grey county, Kitchener-Waterloo and Stratford she gained more guidance and performance experience. She progressed to the provincial level several times, which was another opportunity to develop her skills and meet other outstanding young musicians.

While in high school, Samantha began teaching privately to both violin and piano students. She has also had the opportunity to work as an Assistant Teacher forthe Kincardine Summer Music Festival for the past two years.

Performance experience in the past has included solo work and work with quartets at weddings and other events such as art openings and wine tastings. She has also enjoyed playing as a pick-up muscian for many concerts such as the Benton Street Baptist Church Christmas Festivals, a contribution to the Sweetwater Music Festival with Artistic Director Mark Fewer and a concert with Canadian singer/songwriter, Sarah Slean.

Music isn't Samantha's only passion. High school lead her to realize the beauty of science and biology. She participated in the University of Toronto Nation Biology Exam in 2013 and was ranked in the top 250 (link to article). She gradutated with a certificate of bilingualism (French and English) and a Specialist High Skills Major in Healthcare. She is currently continuing her academic adventure at Western University pursuing a Bachelor of Medical Sciences along with a Performance Diploma in violin.
